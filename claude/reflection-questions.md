# Five Questions Reflection — AI 201: Hero Faction Screen

## Pre-Submission Reflection

Yes, I can defend every major decision in this project. 
The five eras, the models, the color palettes, the 
typography system, the hover behavior, the intro sequence 
text, the context descriptions, the music selection, and 
the overall concept of DÉFILÉ as a living fashion archive 
were all decisions I made before AI touched any code. I 
spent 1 week building my entire context document for my 
project and only 3 days actually coding with Claude's 
assistance. This project reflects my creative direction 
entirely, I came in with a fully formed Design Intent and 
used Claude Code and Gemini as production tools to execute 
my slightly ambitious vision. Every time AI produced 
something that deviated from my intent (such as a spotlight 
that looked like a halo, a character that looked too old, 
an intro sequence that erased text between stages, a 
Christie Brinkley that looked literally like casino art) 
I caught it, rejected it, and redirected it with very 
specific corrections. I verified the work continuously 
throughout the process, checking the browser after every 
change and catching issues that required multiple rounds 
of revision. I understand this project well enough to 
explain how the carousel works, how the CSS filter 
transitions create the desaturation-to-color hover effect, 
how the flash sequence preloads images, how the audio 
system remains in its mute state, and most importantly, 
why each design decision was made. My documentation is 
completely honest, the AI Direction Log and Records of 
Resistance accurately describe what I asked, what AI 
produced, and what I changed or rejected, including the 
moments where the process required multiple rebuilds. 
I do have to include one disclaimer: all the creative 
direction came from me directly into Claude, which in 
return engineered prompts to optimize Claude code and 
prevent me from running out of credits.
